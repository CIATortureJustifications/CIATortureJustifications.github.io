CIA Torture Justifications Post 9/11

CIA & FBI Debates

How internal debates were used to justify Enhanced Interrogation Techniques

After 9/11, for valid reason, there was a huge focus on preventing any future terror attacks in the US United States
. After President George W. Bush’s declaration of the War on Terror, the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 launched their ‘Detention and Interrogation Program’. They did this in attempt to gain further understanding of the global terror network and al-Qaeda in particular. Overall, 119 detainees were admitted into the Program, with around 39 detainees being officially subjected to EITs. After significant intelligence failures prior to 9/11 by the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 in particular which is detailed in the 9/11 Commission Report, they wanted to use these significant extra resources to prevent furture attacks and find those responsible for 9/11, including Osama bin Laden.

Despite intelligence available to the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 to suggest otherwise - the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 stated that Abu Zubaydah was extremely high up in the al-Qaeda hierarchy, possibly being the “third or fourth man”, although this was not the case. This was a factor detailed by Special Agent Ali H. Soufan in his book The Black Banners Subsequently, the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 used this as a main justification for implementing their EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 on Zubaydah as they claimed he was being evasive during interrogations and not giving up information that someone of his stature would possess.

There is evidence (all following evidence it supported in Soufan’s or James E. Mitchell’s book) to suggest that the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 had already decided that the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 had already decided to use EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 as a primary method of interrogation in 2001 prior to supposed traditional rapport interrogation failure. However, in 2002 when the US United States
 began to capture and question detainees official justifications came through inter-agency debates between the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 and FBI Federal Bureau of Investigation
. The FBI Federal Bureau of Investigation
 were strongly advocating for the use of traditional rapport building interrogations, which they claim were harvesting strong, actionable intelligence. Despite the FBI Federal Bureau of Investigation
 gathering intelligence from non-coercive methods. The CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 claimed that these interrogations were not gaining any intelligence, therefore other methods needed to be employed.

The CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 then tasked psychology contractor, Dr. James E. Mitchell with the assistance of Dr. Bruce Jessen to develop an effective interrogation program that would yield significant results. Mitchell believed that he could reverse engineer the US United States
 military’s Survival, Evasion, Resistance, and Escape SERE Survival, Evasion, Resistance, and Escape
 training. SERE Survival, Evasion, Resistance, and Escape
 training is used to train US United States
 personnel who are at high-risk of capture to protect information in the event that they are tortured. As a result, Mitchell developed the following techniques to be used on high-value detainees:

  1. Attention grasp
  2. Walling
  3. Facial hold
  4. Facial (insult) slap
  5. Cramped confinement
  6. Wall standing
  7. Stress positions
  8. Sleep deprivation
  9. Insects place in confinement box
  10. Waterboarding

Mitchell claimed these techniques would be extremely successful at gaining actionable intelligence from detainees. Mitchell convinced the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 that these techniques would particularly be effective against Zubaydah’s precieved deception and evasion. To get these techniques approved the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 repeatedly attempted to discredit FBI Federal Bureau of Investigation
 efforts by stating that FBI Federal Bureau of Investigation
 interrogations were repeatedly failing. Despite the FBI Federal Bureau of Investigation
 proving that they had gained significant actionable intelligence, however, the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 accredited this intelligence to their use of EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 which they had already started using prior to DoJ Department of Justice
 approval. The FBI Federal Bureau of Investigation
 had repeatedly proved through the interrogations conducted by Special Agent Ali H. Soufan that the traditional interrogations had been extremely successful. Despite heavy push back from the FBI Federal Bureau of Investigation
 it seemed the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 had already made a decision that EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 would be used. As the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 controlled the black sites where interrogations using EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 were taking place it became increasingly clear that the FBI Federal Bureau of Investigation
 would not be able to push back enough to prevent the use of EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 being used. As the FBI Federal Bureau of Investigation
 did not want to be associated with these interrogations they left the black sites.
