CIA Torture Justifications Post 9/11

Senate Select Committee on Intelligence

Justifications outlined by the SSCI Study

The SSCI Senate Select Committee on Intelligence
 Study, published in 2014 was a culmination of ten years of research into the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
’s Detetion and Interrogation Program where EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 were employed. The Study was commissioned by the SSCI, which was headed by Dianne Feinstein at the time (pictured below). The research for the Study was conducted by the SSCI itself and led by Daniel J. Jones.

The Study stated that one of the main justifications was that the intelligence services were dedicated more resources to do anything to destroy global terror networks. This would include through the gathering of intelligence of through the interrogation of detainees. It further states how the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 were heavily reliant on the legal justifications gain through the ‘torture memos’ throughout the whole time they were using EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
. CTC Counterterrorism Center
 Director, Cofer Black infers that this is a war and this means there are exceptional circumstances:

The Study found that the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 found as many loopholes as possible in attempt to gain as many justifications as possible. This was so their position on EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 were as strong as possible. As part of these justifications, the Study details how the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 repeatedly misled the DoJ Department of Justice
. One of the most significant instances was misleading the DoJ Department of Justice
 on Abu Zubaydah’s position in al-Qaeda, when they knew it was false - this was instrumental in getting the techniques approved. Even when the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 deviated from what they had informed the DoJ Department of Justice
 of, they then further misled the DoJ in their additional justifications. For example, the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 did not stick to the EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 parameters of what the DoJ Department of Justice
 set, however, the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
’s additional justification was that this was neccessary to save lives. The CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 consistantly, bent the rules of interrogation and what was legal to justify their own actions. The CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 also did not seek reassessment when they deviated from the DoJ Department of Justice
’s regulations.

The Study further outlines the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
’s false claims of EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 effectiveness. Effectiveness was measured based on metrics designed by the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 to make their techniques seem more successful in gaining actionable intelligence. The flawed metrics meant that the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 could further justify the use of EITs as they appeared more effective. The metrics were as follows:

  1. Increased understanding of terrorist networks
  2. Identification of terrorists and those providing material support
  3. Terrorist captures
  4. Terrorist interrogations
  5. Disruption of terrorist operations and financing
  6. Disruption of terrorist recruitment
  7. Reduction in terrorist safe-havens
  8. Development of counterterrorism assets
  9. Intelligence gathering of documents, computer equipment, communications and devices, etc.
  10. Improved information sharing
  11. Improved foreign liaison cooperation against terrorism

The CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 stated that the intelligence gathered from subjecting Abu Zubaydah to EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 met these metrics. This was because the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 accredited information on Khalid Sheikh Mohammed and other terrorist plots to EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 which matched these metrics of success. However, this information was actually obtained through traditional rapport bulding interrogation methods. As the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 accredited the thwarting of terror plots to EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 it added further justification and support to EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 - it was almost a way of making it appear as if the ends justified the means. Clearly, this was just what the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 wanted to make it seem like. The Study states that the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 purposely ommitted information to the DoJ Department of Justice
 and Government to gain justifications for their actions. The SSCI Senate Select Committee on Intelligence
 note further misconduct by the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 as in 2005 CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 Officer, Jose Rodriguez ordered the destruction of videotapes that documented various interrogations, including Abu Zubaydah’s interrogation in particular, which depicted adverse affects he experienced to EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
. When the tapes were requested the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 initially lied about their existance. The destruction took place as it would have raised concerns about EITs efficacy and legitimacy and reduced the strength of EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 justifications. The Study also detailed how no actionable intelligence was obtained through the use of EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 very early on, yet the CIA still decided to use them anyway.

The Study further noted how all information about EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 that was given to external agencies was only checked by the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 and not verified by any other agency meaning that bodies such as the DoJ Department of Justice
 could only make assumptions based on what the CIA Central Intelligence Agency
 claimed. The Study state the CIA were able to do this as they gain vast amounts of unchecked power as during the War on Terror there was a despiration within the intelligence services, the Bush Administration and the nation to bring those responsible for 9/11 to justice and prevent any further plots. These factors were immediately recognised by President Barack Obama in 2009, admitting “we tortured some folks” (as seen in the video below):

On Obama’s second day in office he signed multiple Executive Orders relating to EITs Enhanced Interrogation Techniques
 and ultimately closing down the program. Despite this Obama failed to shut down Guantanemo Bay Detention Camp where some of these detainees including Abu Zubaydah and Khalid Sheikh Mohemmed still remain as of 2025.
